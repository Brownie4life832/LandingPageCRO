**Vertical**: Health & Wellness / Supplements (DTC subscription gummies)

## Headline Formula

**Headline**: "6 Reasons Why Grüns Is Your Gut's New Bestie"
**Angle**: Curiosity / listicle + emotional ("bestie" = relatable, friendly, approachable)
**Word count**: 10 words
**Subheadline**: "Your gut is like your second brain—it affects everything from digestion and energy to mood and immunity. Grüns helps it thrive with 60+ real, nutrient-dense ingredients in a fun-sized snack pack of daily gummies."
**Analysis**: The headline uses the same "6 Reasons" listicle format as Ridge — this appears to be a proven pattern. "Bestie" is a deliberate tone choice targeting a younger, female-leaning audience. The subheadline does heavy lifting: positions gut as important (second brain), names 3 outcome areas (digestion, energy, mood/immunity), states the product mechanism (60+ ingredients), and differentiates the format (gummies, not pills).

- **Customer count**: "1M+ members" — in trust strip, sticky header, and as Reason 07
- **Third-party testing**: "Tested by Light Labs in FEB 2026 — 35 substances tested for quality" (with blue checkmark badge)
- **Review carousel**: "Real Customers. Real Gut Relief." with cards quoting headlines like "They taste great," "Relief within a few days," "Helps clean up my gut"

## The Standout Move

**The sticky header that combines social proof + CTA in a persistent bar.** Most sticky headers just show a logo and nav. Grüns puts "4.8 stars · 85K+ reviews · 1M+ members" PLUS the green CTA button in the sticky bar. This means:
1. Every scroll position has both trust AND a conversion path visible
2. The social proof compounds — you see those numbers dozens of times during a single page visit
3. The CTA is always one click away but never feels pushy because it's embedded in trust signals

This is the most efficient use of sticky header space I've seen.

## Steal List

- **Sticky header = social proof + CTA** — Put your best trust numbers AND your primary CTA in the persistent top bar. Two birds, one bar.
- **"Seriously" parenthetical copy** — "Better poops (seriously)" breaks formality and signals authenticity. Using casual parentheticals in headlines builds trust with younger audiences.
- **Category labels on reasons** — Each section tagged with uppercase category (DIGESTION, ENERGY, IMMUNITY) + number. Creates a magazine-editorial feel that elevates perceived quality.
- **Over-deliver on the promise** — The headline says "6 Reasons" but delivers 7+. Exceeding expectations builds trust subconsciously.
- **Per-day price framing** — "$1.46 per day" next to the $40.80 price. For subscription products, the daily cost makes the total feel negligible.
- **Lab testing with specifics** — "Tested by Light Labs in FEB 2026 — 35 substances tested" is more believable than generic "third-party tested." Dates and numbers = credibility.
- **Discount auto-applied badge** — "Limited Time Discount Auto-Applied ✅" in a yellow banner below CTA. Removes friction ("do I need a code?") and creates reciprocity feeling.
- **No navigation on LP** — Zero links to homepage, categories, or anything else. The only choices are scroll, click CTA, or leave. This focus maximizes conversion.
