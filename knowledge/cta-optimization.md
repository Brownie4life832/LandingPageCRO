# CTA Optimization — Best Practices

## CTA Copy That Converts

### Principles
1. **Start with a verb**: "Get", "Start", "Download", "Join", "Try"
2. **Communicate value, not action**: "Get My Free Report" beats "Download"
3. **Use first person**: "Start My Free Trial" outperforms "Start Your Free Trial"
4. **Reduce perceived risk**: Add "Free", "No credit card", "Cancel anytime"
5. **Create urgency honestly**: "Limited spots", "Offer ends Friday"

### High-Converting CTA Examples
| Weak | Strong | Why It's Better |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Submit | Get My Free Guide | Value-focused |
| Sign Up | Start Growing Today | Outcome-focused |
| Learn More | See How It Works | Specific next step |
| Buy Now | Start My Free Trial | Lower commitment |
| Click Here | Get Instant Access | Communicates benefit |
| Register | Join 10,000+ Members | Social proof built in |

## CTA Design Rules

### Color & Contrast
- CTA button must have the highest visual contrast on the page
- Don't match it to the nav or secondary elements
- Test: squint at the page — the CTA should still stand out

### Size & Shape
- Minimum 44x44px touch target (mobile)
- Desktop: large enough to be unmissable, not so large it feels desperate
- Rounded corners (4-8px) typically outperform sharp corners
- Padding: at least 12px vertical, 24px horizontal

### Placement
- Above the fold: always have a CTA visible without scrolling
- After key benefit sections: place CTAs at natural decision points
- End of page: capture visitors who read everything
- Sticky/floating CTA on mobile for long pages

### Supporting Elements
- **Microcopy below CTA**: "No credit card required" / "Setup in 2 minutes"
- **Directional cues**: Arrows, eye gaze, or visual flow pointing to CTA
- **Contrast with background**: Button should pop against its section

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Competing CTAs**: Multiple equal-weight CTAs confuse visitors. One primary, one ghost/text secondary max.
2. **CTA below the fold only**: Don't make visitors hunt for the action.
3. **Generic copy on every CTA**: Vary CTA text contextually ("See Pricing" after features, "Start Free Trial" after testimonials).
4. **Hiding the CTA in navigation**: Nav links are navigation, not conversion.
5. **Color that blends in**: If your brand is blue and your CTA is blue, it disappears.
