# Headlines & Value Propositions — Best Practices

## Headline Formulas That Convert

### 1. Outcome-Focused
"[Achieve outcome] without [pain point]"
- Example: "Grow Revenue 3x Without Hiring More Sales Reps"

### 2. Specific + Timeframe
"[Get specific result] in [timeframe]"
- Example: "Launch Your Online Store in Under 10 Minutes"

### 3. Comparison/Switch
"The [category] that [key differentiator]"
- Example: "The CRM That Actually Gets Used By Your Team"

### 4. Social Proof Headline
"[Number] [audience] use [product] to [outcome]"
- Example: "12,000 Marketers Use Acme to Double Their Email Revenue"

### 5. Problem-Agitation
"Stop [pain point]. Start [desired outcome]."
- Example: "Stop Losing Leads to Slow Follow-Up. Start Closing in Minutes."

## Common Headline Mistakes

1. **Company-centric**: "We're the #1 Platform for..." (nobody cares about you)
2. **Vague**: "Transform Your Business" (how? with what?)
3. **Feature-first**: "AI-Powered Analytics Dashboard" (so what?)
4. **Jargon-heavy**: "Leverage Synergistic Solutions" (meaningless)
5. **Too long**: Headlines over 12 words lose impact
6. **No specificity**: "Better Results, Faster" (claims without proof)

## Value Proposition Checklist

A strong value prop answers three questions:
1. **What do you do?** (in plain language)
2. **For whom?** (specific audience)
3. **Why should they care?** (concrete outcome)

### Value Prop Structure
- **Headline**: One sentence, core benefit (6-12 words)
- **Subheadline**: How you deliver the benefit (15-25 words)
- **Bullet points**: 3 supporting benefits or features-as-benefits
- **Visual**: Screenshot, demo, or hero image that reinforces the message

## Testing Framework for Headlines

When suggesting A/B tests:
- Test ONE element at a time (outcome vs. pain point, specific vs. general)
- Minimum sample: 1,000 visitors per variation
- Run for at least 2 full business cycles (typically 2 weeks)
- Measure: bounce rate, scroll depth, CTA click-through
