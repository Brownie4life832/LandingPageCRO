# Social Proof & Trust Signals — Best Practices

## Types of Social Proof (by impact)

### 1. Customer Results (Highest Impact)
- Specific, quantified outcomes: "Increased conversions by 47% in 30 days"
- Before/after comparisons
- Revenue or time saved figures
- Include context: company size, industry, timeframe

### 2. Testimonials
**Strong testimonial checklist:**
- [ ] Full name (not "J.S." or "Anonymous")
- [ ] Photo (real, not stock)
- [ ] Title and company
- [ ] Specific result or outcome mentioned
- [ ] Addresses a common objection
- [ ] Under 3 sentences

**Weak testimonials to flag:**
- "Great product!" (no specifics)
- No attribution (no name, no photo)
- Only praise, no substance
- Obviously written by marketing

### 3. Logos & Partnerships
- Place client logos near the hero section or just below it
- Use 4-8 logos (too many reduces impact)
- Include recognizable brands when possible
- "Trusted by" or "Used by" framing
- Grayscale logos look more professional than full color

### 4. Numbers & Scale
- User/customer counts: "Join 50,000+ teams"
- Usage stats: "3M+ reports generated"
- Years in business (if impressive)
- Team size or global presence (B2B)

### 5. Third-Party Validation
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot ratings with star visuals
- Awards and recognitions
- Press mentions ("As seen in...")
- Industry certifications
- Security badges (SOC 2, GDPR, ISO)

### 6. Real-Time Social Proof
- "X people are viewing this right now"
- Recent sign-up notifications
- Live user counts
- Use sparingly and honestly — fake urgency destroys trust

## Trust Signal Placement

| Section | Best Trust Signals |
|---------|-------------------|
| Hero / Above Fold | Logo bar, user count, rating badge |
| After Features | Customer testimonials with results |
| Near Pricing | Guarantee badge, security seals |
| Near Form/CTA | Privacy statement, "no spam" microcopy |
| Footer | Certifications, compliance badges |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Testimonials on a separate page**: Put them where decisions happen
2. **Logo overload**: 20+ logos dilutes impact
3. **No specifics in proof**: "We love it!" doesn't convince anyone
4. **Fake reviews**: Visitors can detect inauthenticity — it backfires
5. **Missing trust near conversion points**: Forms without privacy reassurance lose submissions
6. **Outdated dates**: "Best of 2019" signals neglect, not quality
