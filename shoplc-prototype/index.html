The Shop LC Gemstone Guide

Learn what makes each stone special — then shop the collection.

Tanzanite

Found only near Mt. Kilimanjaro, tanzanite is 1,000x rarer than diamond. Its violet-blue trichroism shifts color with every angle. Supply may be depleted within 20 years.

Mohs Hardness: 6.5 · Origin: Tanzania Shop Tanzanite →

Lab-Grown Diamond

Chemically, physically, and optically identical to mined diamonds — created in a controlled environment. Same brilliance, same hardness, a fraction of the price.

Mohs Hardness: 10 · Origin: Laboratory Shop Lab Diamond →

Sapphire

The stone of royalty. Ceylon sapphires are prized for their cornflower blue hue. One of the hardest natural gemstones, making it perfect for everyday rings.

Mohs Hardness: 9 · Origin: Sri Lanka, Myanmar Shop Sapphire →

Emerald

Prized since Cleopatra's time. Fine emeralds can command higher prices per carat than diamonds. Natural inclusions (called "jardin") are expected and add character.

Mohs Hardness: 7.5 · Origin: Colombia, Zambia Shop Emerald →

Morganite

Named after J.P. Morgan, this blush-pink beryl has surged in popularity. Its warm tone flatters every skin tone and pairs beautifully with rose gold settings.

Mohs Hardness: 7.5 · Origin: Brazil, Madagascar Shop Morganite →

Opal

No two opals are alike. Ethiopian opals display a stunning play-of-color — flashes of red, green, blue, and orange dancing across the surface as the stone moves.

Mohs Hardness: 5.5 · Origin: Ethiopia, Australia Shop Opal →
